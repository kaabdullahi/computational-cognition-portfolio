# Computational Cognition Portfolio
### Khalid Abdullahi | MClin Student | Institute of Neuroscience, Australia

## Contents
- /honours-thesis — Original honours analysis. Bayesian RM-ANOVA (R) testing 
  scheduling performance across team/separate conditions. Includes the original 
  sampling-distribution simulation (MATLAB, A. Eidels) used to benchmark 
  distance-from-optimality against chance.
- /ddm-analysis — EZ-diffusion reanalysis of honours data: team vs individual 
  decision-making (R).
  Finding 1: teams show significantly worse scheduling optimality than individuals 
  (F(1) = 117.52, p < .001, d = 0.61, BF_inclusion > 10 — Bayesian RM-ANOVA 
  from original thesis).
  Finding 2: EZ-diffusion reveals no difference in drift rate, boundary separation, 
  or non-decision time between conditions (all p > .30, all d < 0.25) — the team 
  deficit is localised to the choice-selection stage, not the within-RDK decision 
  process. Supports a coordination account over a cognitive capacity account of 
  team performance loss.

differences as one application of broader individual-differences research.

## Key Results to Date

### Honours Thesis (Abdullahi & Eidels, 2024)
- Individuals choose significantly more optimal task sequences than teams
  (F(1) = 117.52, p < .001, d = 0.61, BF_inclusion > 10)
- Teams forced into harder choices by co-operator lockout mechanism
  (choice difficulty: M = 3.84 vs M = 4.34, d = 0.52)
- Workload capacity limited in teams: C(t) = .93, BF = 4,166

### EZ-Diffusion Extension (2026)
- Drift rate, boundary separation, and non-decision time equivalent
  across Team and Separate conditions (all p > .30, all d < 0.25)
- Team scheduling deficit localised to choice-selection stage,
  not within-RDK decision process
- Supports coordination account over cognitive capacity account
